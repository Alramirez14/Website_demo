The smith river watershed

Located approximately 40 miles northeast of Reedsport, Oregon, The Smith River and its tributaries are home to a variety of species crucial to ecosystem function in Oregon's Coast Range; primarily an endangered stock of Coho Salmon that return to its tributaries to spawn each year.

The sites

Oregon State University in collaboration with the US Forest Service has established four (4) study sites in tributary streams to the Smith River: Beaver Creek, North Sister Creek, Panther Creek, and Halfway Creek. Chemical sampling for metals and anions began in August of 2024, collocated with the above study sites as well as at an additional site on the main Smith River downstream of Panther and Halfway Creeks.

Study Sites

Beaver Creek

Our northwesternmost site, Beaver Creek is a stream with a high gradient that carves its way down a sandy ravine, full of Western Redcedar, Douglas Fir, and various ferns.

North Sister Creek

At relatively high elevation compared to other sites, North Sister Creek is a wide, shallow stream with lots of exposed bedrock. Despite this, cover and woody derbris make this a perferred home for macroinvertabrates and fish species.

Halfway Creek

A lower elevation stream, Halfway Creek converges with Smith River from the south side. Recent restorative work from the Smith River Watershed Council introducing structure into the stream channel has created new habitat for Coastal Cutthroat Trout.

Panther Creek

This sample site is located beneath a bridge at the easternmost reach of the study area. Annual flood events make channel migration a common occurence at this site, which has led to a need to regularly, manually reset the channel bounds.
