The smith river watershed

Located approximately 40 miles northeast of Reedsport, Oregon, The Smith River and its tributaries are home to a variety of species crucial to ecosystem function in Oregon's Coast Range; primarily an endangered stock of Coho Salmon that return to its tributaries to spawn each year.

More intriguing information

Oregon State University in collaboration with the US Forest Service has established four (4) study sites in tributary streams to the Smith River: Beaver Creek, North Sister Creek, Panther Creek, and Halfway Creek. Chemical sampling for metals and anions began in August of 2024, collocated with the above study sites as well as at an additional site on the main Smith River downstream of Panther and Halfway Creeks.
